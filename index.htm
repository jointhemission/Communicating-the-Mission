Neuroinformatics & Science Communication

Bridging neuroscience, data, and understanding

I have a background in neuroinformatics, working at the intersection of neuroscience, data analysis, and computational modeling. My interests focus on how complex neural systems can be understood, modeled, and communicated through data-driven approaches.

Beyond research and technology, I am deeply interested in science communication. I believe that scientific knowledge should not remain confined to academia, but should be accessible, transparent, and meaningful to a wider audience.

My mission is to communicate science as a living process — one that connects theory, data, and human curiosity — and to help translate complex scientific ideas into clear and engaging narratives.
